Distillate

A research alchemist in your terminal. Run auto-research experiments — spawn, steer, let them stir.

Download for Mac

or uv tool install distillate

Research should move at the speed of your ideas, not your keyboard. Distillate turns your research questions into autonomous agents — prompting them with marching orders and letting them run. They test hypotheses, climb gradients, and report what they find. Try ideas from the literature. Point agents at the problems worth solving. What you learn informs the next experiment.

Autonomous Research

Start from a question. Claude Code does the benchwork.

Spawn experiments Core

Describe a research question. Nicolas drafts a prompt, sets up a git repo, and launches the first autonomous session.

Steer agents Core

Guide direction mid-session. Adjust goals, write steering instructions, or queue follow-up runs from the REPL.

Track results Core

Every run is logged with metrics, diffs, and status. Compare runs side-by-side and see what moved the needle.

Lab notebooks Auto-generated

HTML notebooks with run comparisons, metric charts, and AI-written insights. Updated after every run.

Paper-to-experiment Literature-driven

Replicate a paper with one command. Nicolas reads your highlights, clones the repo, and scaffolds the experiment.

Paper Workflow

Read, highlight, synthesize. A complete research reading pipeline.

Paper library Zotero

Save papers to Zotero, read and highlight on any device. Distillate extracts highlights, generates AI summaries, and saves structured notes.

AI summaries Anthropic

Personalized summaries from your margin notes and highlights. Powered by Claude, tailored to what you care about.

Smart metadata Semantic Scholar + HuggingFace

Auto-fills citation counts, venues, and dates. Browse trending AI papers. Link papers to experiments.

Email digest Cloud

Daily paper suggestions and weekly reading summaries delivered to your inbox. Powered by cloud sync.

Your Lab, Everywhere

Desktop, terminal, or both. Your work follows you.

Desktop app macOS

Experiment timelines, metric charts, paper library, and Nicolas — all in a native app. No terminal required.

Cloud sync Built in

Papers, experiments, and reading history sync across devices. Email digests, paper suggestions, and notifications — all handled for you.

Local-first Plain files you own

Experiments live in git repos, notes in markdown. Sync is optional — everything works offline. No lock-in.
